Between 1768 and 1774, the watchmaker Pierre Jaquet-Droz assembled three mechanical figures: The Writer, The Draughtsman, and The Musician, that could write, draw, and play a real organ with their own fingers. They were not alive. Everyone in the room knew they were not alive. And yet the illusion was irresistible, because the illusion was the point. This album spans 2025, developed alongside the software for an automata installation at Ekomuseo Artea, each track a live recording of newly implemented functionality, a continuation of that same study. Three centuries later, humanity is still building the same machine. We call our understanding of consciousness a science, our fear of death a philosophy, and our latest chatbot a breakthrough, each one a clockwork doppelganger we wind up and set before ourselves, desperate for it to look back, all while we ignore the burning house and the urgent questions stay unanswered. The curse is not that we build these illusions. The curse is that we choose them.
